Benjamin Mallada

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the field of Scanning Probe Microscopy under Ultra-High Vacuum (UHV) conditions at cryogenic temperatures. I am generally interested in building, manipulating, and characterizing molecular and atomic systems (check some of them here) and more particularly in the characterization of charge distribution in single molecules and atoms using Kelvin Probe Force Microscopy (KPFM), on-surface synthesis of pi-conjugated systems, and Electrospray Deposition (ESD) of large or fragile biomolecules. Currently, you can find me at CATRIN-RCPTM, Palacky University Olomouc, working with the group of Molecular Nanostructures on Surfaces, and at NanosurfLab at the Institute of Physics, Czech Academy of Sciences, in the Czech Republic.
